Reflections

Vitualisation

When I was working as a Data Analyst, my manager often questioned me about the visualisations I presented. The questions were about the colour of the graphs, the types of graphs, and the reasons why I used specific statistical methods. These sessions sometimes felt more like interrogations than discussions, which caused me stress and anxiety before the meetings. A positive aspect of this situation was that I was able to build relationships with my colleagues, as I often asked them to review my presentation beforehand. I also learned to prepare my graphs more thoroughly, understand my audience’s point of view, and make the presentations as simple as possible. A negative aspect of this situation was that it made me doubt my graphs and affected my confidence.

My manager’s questions were simply to ensure that my data was accurate, clear, and that he fully understood what I was trying to convey, so he could make informed decisions based on my presentation. At times, my presentation wasn’t very smooth and could cause confusion about the points I was trying to communicate. My manager also preferred the graphs to be presented in the same format each month, so he could easily understand them at a glance. I now understand how important visualisation is. I can prepare the graphs to be both simple and insightful, using straightforward terms and being ready to explain them clearly.
