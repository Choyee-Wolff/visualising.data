Colour

I found a study showing that the colors blue and red can influence stakeholdersâ€™ perceptions, (Elvira Anna Graziano, Lucia Leonelli, GianMario Raggetti, 2021) which I found fascinating. It highlighted that in Western cultures, red often signifies danger or negativity, while in Chinese, red colour possesses the semantical meaning of a happiness and joy. (N. S. Kutuzova, 2020) This cultural difference was intriguing because it affects how colours should be used in presentations or dashboards depending on the audience. The positive aspect of this discovery was that it made me realise that colour choices can have significant impacts on stakeholders' perceptions and reactions. On the downside, I now see that I might have unintentionally used colors in a way that could be misinterpreted by some audiences, highlighting an area for improvement in my design approach.

The challenge lies in adapting visual designs to accommodate different cultural perspectives. I learned that cultural context is crucial when it comes to color selection in visualisations. I need to be mindful of the audience's background and adapt my design choices accordingly. This experience has made me more aware of the importance of cultural sensitivity in visual communication. In the future, I will explore resources on color psychology and cultural differences to enhance my design skills and ensure that my visualisations are effective and culturally appropriate.
